Instructions


A) Add Input Point

Do you have something to communicate about recovery in your community? Does it fall within the categories of:

  • Organization/Community - Name the community you come from and that you are speaking for on the Recovery Needs Shareabouts
  • Community Asset/Valued Landmark - What do you and your community value? Is there a building or open space that you want to see preserved or restored? What are local traditions or annual events and where do they occur? 
  • Need/Vulnerability - Where was your community hardest hit by the storm? What do you think needs to be strengthened to increase community capacity and resilience to future disasters?
  • Assistance Request - How can a government of NGO help your community? Certain workshops in capacity building, wetland restoration, local economic development, insurance, or mapping tools? Give recommendations! 
  • Miscellaneous -  What has been overlooked? What is obstructing recovery in your community? What does your community need for resiliency?        


You should contribute! 


To find the geographic location of the point you want to add, click and hold on the map, then drag the map over to the location that you are seeking. You can zoom in and out using the normal tricks of your personal computer or phone (ie. dragging two fingers across the mouse pad on a mac), or use the zoom in and out buttons in the upper left corner of the map window. 

You will see a the map cursor hovering over the map, when it is over your desired location, click the "Add Here" button in the lower left corner of the map window. A pop-up window will appear. Fill this out. 

Name your Input - give the landmark…need…request for assistance a descriptive name, or identify the name of the organization or community you want to add to the map. 

Identify the best match for input type and topic focus. Add an image if you have a good descriptive photograph or drawing. It is important to give details in your description. Give contextual information, and how you imagine going forward to coordinate recovery and address issues. Is anyone already working on it? Do you have any studies you suggest reading related to the topic? Do you know who should be contacted for more information about the item added to the map? 

Lastly, please add your name so we know who is participating. Add your email if you want to be contacted to follow-up on your map input. By adding your email, it does not imply that you will definitely be contacted.
